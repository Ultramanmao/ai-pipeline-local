# 8 GB Local LLM Benchmark: Five Models, Six Tests, One Question — Does Speed Beat Size?

> Date: 2026-07-22 · GPU: RTX 5060 Laptop 8GB (sm_120) · CUDA: cu132 · Framework: llama.cpp · OS: Windows 11 native
> Release: https://github.com/Ultramanmao/ai-pipeline-local/releases/tag/v14.0

## TL;DR

This release moves away from diffusion pipelines and onto the LLM side of the stack: **five local models** benchmarked head-to-head on a single 8 GB RTX 5060 Laptop via `llama.cpp`.

Six tasks per model (code, translation, classical Chinese, math, logic, general knowledge). 30 scored runs across three iterations. Two key findings flip the usual wisdom:

1. **The fastest model isn't the weakest.** Gemma-4-12B at **40.9 t/s** scores 49/60 — within two points of the heaviest model (Qwen3.6-35B, 51/60). On an 8 GB card, fast and capable are not mutually exclusive.
2. **The gap between "big" and "small" models is mostly thinking depth, not reasoning ability.** With the thinking process stripped out (512 tokens, no-CoT prompt), Qwen3.6, Gemma-4-12B, GLM-4.7, and Gemma4-26B all collapse to **49–51 points** — the same tier. The apparent lead of larger models is inflated by the reasoning budget.

**The verdict:** for daily use on an 8 GB card, Gemma-4-12B (6.3 GB, 40.9 t/s) is the sweet spot. Qwen3.6-35B wins on depth work but runs 2× slower at 19 t/s. Three of the five tested models are effectively dead weight.

## Three Iterations: What the Numbers Actually Show

### Iteration 1 — CoT mode, `-n 256`

Result: **every model broke.** 256 tokens isn't enough for a reasoning trace. All five ate their budget in `[Start thinking]` tags. Nobody delivered a complete answer.

One bright spot: GLM-4.7-Flash produced an oracle-bone etymology for "习" (practice as in a bird practicing flight) that actually held up — Chinese-language depth is real. But it still ran out of tokens on code.

**Scores (CoT, 256 tokens):**

| Model | Code | Trans | 文言 | Math | Logic | Knowledge | Total |
|-------|------|-------|------|------|-------|-----------|-------|
| GLM-4.7-Flash | 6 | 8 | **9** | 6 | **7** | **9** | **45** |
| Qwen3.6-35B | 7 | 8 | 7 | 6 | 6 | 8 | 41 |
| Gemma-4-12B | 5 | 8 | 8 | 5 | 4 | 6 | 36 |
| Gemma4-26B-A4B | 4 | 7 | 7 | 5 | 4 | 7 | 34 |

Fastest model (Gemma-4-12B) ranked second-to-last. Conclusion so far: size wins, speed loses. This is the conventional wisdom. The next iteration disproves it.

### Iteration 2 — No-COT mode, `-n 512`

System prompt: *"Output ONLY the final answer. No thinking, no reasoning, no explanation."*

Scores shifted dramatically:

| Model | Code | Trans | 文言 | Math | Logic | Knowledge | Total |
|-------|------|-------|------|------|-------|-----------|-------|
| Qwen3.6-35B | 7 | 9 | 8 | **10** | 7 | **10** | **51** |
| Gemma-4-12B | **9** | 9 | 8 | **10** | 8 | 5 | **49** |
| GLM-4.7-Flash | 4 | 9 | **9** | 9 | 8 | **10** | **49** |
| Gemma4-26B-A4B | 7 | 9 | 7 | **10** | 8 | 8 | **49** |

**Four models within two points of each other.** The 9-point lead GLM-4.7 held in CoT mode evaporates (45 → 49). The 13-point gap between Qwen3.6 and Gemma-4-12B shrinks to 2 points (41 → 51 vs 36 → 49).

**What changed (CoT → No-COT delta):**

| Model | CoT | No-COT | Delta |
|-------|-----|--------|-------|
| Gemma-4-12B | 36 | 49 | **+13** |
| Gemma4-26B-A4B | 34 | 49 | **+15** |
| Qwen3.6-35B | 41 | 51 | +10 |
| GLM-4.7-Flash | 45 | 49 | +4 |

The smaller models gained the most — meaning their raw capability is comparable to the larger models; they were just starved of reasoning budget. **Larger models don't reason better; they reason *longer*.**

### Iteration 3 — Bonsai-27B special case (`-n 1024`)

Bonsai-27B-Q1_0 is the outlier: 3.6 GB, 1-bit quantization, 38.1 t/s speed. At 512 tokens it was unusable (thought process ate the budget). With 1024 tokens and a hardened system prompt:

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Code | 7 | Complete, runnable quicksort + example |
| Translation | 8 | Delivered the translation |
| 文言 | 3 | Still outputs English |
| Math | 9 | Correct answer: 23 chickens, 12 rabbits |
| Logic | 8 | Complete reasoning, found the cycle |
| General knowledge | **1** | Invented "诸葛子" — pure hallucination |

1-bit quantization lets it run fast and fit anywhere, but general knowledge is garbage. **A viable draft tool, not a general-purpose model.**

## Four Findings That Matter

### 1. Speed and capability are decoupled on 8 GB

Gemma-4-12B (6.3 GB, pure GPU) achieves both 40.9 t/s **and** 49/60. The old tradeoff — fast models are dumb, capable models are slow — no longer holds at the 8–12B scale. Pick Gemma-4-12B for daily use and accept that you lose depth work, not competence.

### 2. "Big model advantage" is mostly reasoning budget

The 9-point CoT gap between GLM-4.7 and Gemma-4-12B vanishes in no-COT mode. This isn't unique to Chinese models — it reflects how `llama.cpp`'s thinking tags consume the token budget. **When benchmarking local models, fix the reasoning length, or the results are about who thinks longest, not who reasons best.**

### 3. 1-bit quantization is a real trap

Bonsai-27B-Q1_0's 1 point on general knowledge isn't noise — it's a structural failure of Q1_0 quantization at general facts. Math and logic (which are procedure-based, not memory-based) survive. Knowledge retrieval does not. **Don't use Q1_0 quantization for anything that requires real-world knowledge.**

### 4. 512 tokens isn't enough for logic

Every model truncated the three-person liar puzzle before reaching the conclusion. Real-world prompt engineering on local models needs **1024+ max tokens**, not 256.
